NSDA RECORDING

The National Speech and Debate Association (NSDA) is the world's largest and oldest interscholastic speech and debate honor society. Those who are members receive an array of benefits ranging from Speech material, tournament opportunities, achievement recognition, and eligibility for national awards including Exemplary Service, Student of the Year, Academic All American, and more. TJHSST is a member school of NSDA, and as such, members of Model United Nations, Debate, and Drama are eligible to join the NSDA for a fee of $20. This one-time membership fee will cover you for your entire high school speech and debate career. Upon joining, members will report tournament records to Mrs. Bakke that can date back to freshman year, who will then document those tournament/conference records as NSDA points. 

Acquiring NSDA points by attending tournaments, serving/volunteering on oral speaking events, and performing well will allow members to attain levels of distinctions and awards. NSDA has eight levels of distinction along with the prestigious Academic All American award that students can achieve in the junior year upon reaching 750 points. You may also earn points for non-judged events (e.g. MUN mocks or debate in houses), but you must report the description, number of speeches, duration of speaking time, number in attendance. For judged events, you are expected to record your ranks/scores.
